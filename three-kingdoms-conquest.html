📖 The Story So Far

Why is everyone fighting?

For four hundred years the Han dynasty ruled a united China. But by the late second century the court is rotten: child emperors sit the throne while eunuchs and in-laws sell offices, famine stalks the villages, and taxes feed palaces instead of granaries. When a faith healer named Zhang Jiao promises a new golden age, hundreds of thousands tie yellow scarves around their heads and rise in revolt. The dynasty survives the rebellion — but only by handing real armies to provincial governors and ambitious volunteers. The court never gets those armies back.

A brutal frontier general, Dong Zhuo, seizes the capital and the boy emperor. The warlords who unite to stop him discover something more dangerous than any tyrant: that the empire is now simply whoever has the most soldiers. When the coalition dissolves, a generation of governors, adventurers, and opportunists begins carving the realm apart.

Out of that chaos, three powers slowly crush or absorb all the others. Wei in the north, built by the brilliant and ruthless Cao Cao, who controls the emperor and speaks with his voice. Shu-Han in the western riverlands, founded by Liu Bei, a sandal-weaver of distant imperial blood who claims to be restoring the Han. Wu in the south, the river kingdom of the Sun family, defended by the Yangtze and the finest fleets in the world. Each believes it is the legitimate heir of the empire. Each needs the other two gone.

The goal — in history and in this game — is the same: unite the land. One realm, one banner, an end to a century of war. Every campaign battle you fight is one step of that long road.

The three sagas

蜀 The Shu Saga follows Liu Bei and his sworn brothers Guan Yu and Zhang Fei from the Oath in the Peach Garden — three volunteers with nothing but loyalty — through exile, alliance, and kingdom-building, to Zhuge Liang's desperate Northern Expeditions. It is the story of the underdog: virtue against power.

魏 The Wei Saga follows Cao Cao from a lone pursuit no one else dared, through the miracle at Guandu against odds of ten to one, to an empire that must then be defended from genius, rebellion, and its own court. It is the story of the builder: order through strength.

吳 The Wu Saga follows three generations of the Sun family — the Tiger, the Little Conqueror, and the patient younger brother — who turn the wild riverlands into a kingdom and burn the greatest fleet ever assembled at the Red Cliffs. It is the story of the survivor: the river holds.

These stories are dramatized from the historical Three Kingdoms period (184–280 CE), one of the most retold eras in world literature.

❓ How to Play

🎯 The goal

Conquer every enemy city on the map. Cities recruit troops over time (up to a cap). Neutral gray cities don't grow — they're the easiest early conquests. When the enemy owns no cities and has no armies marching, you win.

🖱 Controls & the chain attack

Drag from one of your cities to any city and release — half your troops march there. The chain: press on city A, sweep through B, C, D… (all yours), release on the target: every linked city attacks at once. This is how you crack big garrisons. Use the ×-button in battle (or the menu setting) to slow the game to 0.5× or speed it to 2×.

⭐ Stars & par time

Win fast for 3 stars, moderately fast for 2, any win earns 1. Stars unlock generals (up to 33★ for the legends). Replay old battles to improve your stars anytime.

✦ Merit & the War Council

Every battle pays merit — wins pay big (first win of the day is 2×, streaks add bonuses), and even defeats salvage +10. Spend merit at the 🏛 War Council on permanent upgrades: recruit speed, march speed, attack, and starting troops. If you're stuck, grind 2–3 battles, buy an upgrade, and try again. Your leaderboard rank uses lifetime merit and never drops when you spend.

👑 Generals, XP & duo bonds

Each kingdom fields six generals with different passives. Your commanding general earns XP every battle and their perk grows +5% per level (max Lv10). Unlock certain pairs and their duo bond adds a second bonus — see the Generals screen.

👹 Bosses & difficulty tiers

Every 10th battle is a boss with a crowned city and a special ability (announced in the briefing — plan around it!). Difficulty runs on 10 tiers from Recruit to Legend, ramping smoothly across the 200-battle campaign. Watch the tier chip at the top of every battle.

Bosses flee! When a warlord senses his city is about to fall, he evacuates with every soldier under a great banner and seizes a new stronghold — the crown, portrait, and boss ring move with him. Cut down the fleeing column, or chase him city to city until nowhere is left to run.

✨ Special cities (Wonders)

Medium and larger maps contain neutral special cities marked with a pulsing gold ring, an icon, and their name. Capture one and its power serves you — lose it and the enemy benefits instead. The names tell you what they do:

🏮 Great Beacon — your cities recruit 15% faster · ⛏ Iron Mine — attacks hit 12% harder · 🐎 Horse Ranch — armies march 20% faster · 🌾 Grand Granary — all cities hold +15 troops · 🥁 War Drum Tower — every order sends 10% more troops · ⛩ Twin Gates — attacks from this city launch a second wave · 💧 Sacred Spring — this city recruits twice as fast and holds +40 · ✴ Caltrop Fields — enemy armies march 15% slower · 🛡 Stone Bastion — attacks against your cities are 10% weaker · 🪖 Levy Grounds — all your cities gain +2 troops every 15s · 🪙 Imperial Mint — win while holding it for +25 bonus merit.

The battle briefing lists which special cities are on the map. How many can appear is set by the game admin (default: 1 per map).

🗺 Weekly Challenge & badges

One shared map per week — everyone races the same battlefield for the fastest clear (top times on the Leaderboard screen). Badges and crowns are earned for campaign milestones, streaks, and feats; show them off on your profile.

🔒 Protecting your commander

Set a passcode on the login screen and nobody else can log into your name. Forgot it? The game admin can clear it from the separate Admin Console (admin.html).
